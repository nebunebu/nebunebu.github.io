About

Online I tend to go by either nebu or nebunebu, stylized in lowercases, depending on username availibility. I did a little write up on the name here.

In 2018, I completed an undergraduate degree in analytic philosophy. I earned a secondary major in the humanities - not the study of western civilization, but a build your own major program, where mine consisted primarily of courses that would have been toward an English major.

For roughly a year after graduating, I resold used books. In short, I would go to thrift stores and library sales and resell them online. It was a lot of fun.

Between doing that and up until the start of the pandemic, I worked as a substitute teacher.

Roughly a year ago, I started learning to code. After college I had somewhat regretted not pursing a stem degree, and moreover not having had taken more math courses.

On some job board, I saw an ad for one of those places that pay minumum wage to teach you to code and then contract you out to companies. It caught my attention.

Researching it, everything I saw recommended against it saying that bootcamps, were preferable. So I started researching bootcamps. Rather than going to one, I decided to just learn with open curricula. Initially this was with App Academy Open's ruby curriculum.

I would not say I got to far with it - far enough to learn the basics of the ruby language, but not enough to develop rails apps.

After that my interests have diverted to quite a few different places, and in the course of doing so, I have picked up the fundamentals of a few different languages.
